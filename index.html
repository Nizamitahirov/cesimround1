Case Summary

The US Department of Justice has dismantled a long-standing cartel in the mobile handset industry, forcing all major manufacturers, including Mobilé Inc., to replace their top management. The years of collusion resulted in technological stagnation and artificially high prices. As the new leadership, Team Databyte is tasked with navigating this newly competitive landscape. We start with a strong financial position but face the immense challenge of fostering innovation, optimizing a global supply chain, and competing aggressively across US, European, and Asian markets to maximize shareholder value.

Executive Summary

This report details the foundational strategic decisions executed by Team Databyte for Mobilé Inc. in Round 1. Our approach was built on three pillars: Financial Fortification, Product Supremacy, and Strategic Expansion.

We addressed a critical liquidity crisis by securing $700 million in long-term debt to eliminate all high-cost emergency loans. Operationally, we focused all resources on a technologically superior "Tech 1" product with 4 features, after data analysis revealed "Tech 2" to be unviable this round.

As a result, Mobilé Inc. was transformed from a financially vulnerable entity into a stable, globally-poised competitor with a $694 million cash reserve and a market-leading product.

A New Reality and Our Mission

We took the helm of Mobilé Inc. at a historic crossroads. The challenge was not merely to maintain the status quo—a product of a non-competitive market—but to fundamentally rebuild the company's culture and operations around three core principles:

  • Agility: To build an organization capable of reacting instantly to market shifts.
  • Innovation: To embed product development into the company's DNA.
  • Efficiency: To optimize every operational process to minimize costs and maximize profit.

Our sole objective is to maximize long-term cumulative shareholder return. This report details the first, decisive steps toward that goal.

Deep Dive: Our Rationale

Decision: We focused all sales efforts on "Tech 1" and rejected "Tech 2" for this round. We immediately licensed 2 new features for both technologies and initiated in-house development for a future "Tech 2" feature.

Rationale: While market sentiment was high for "Tech 2," hard data from network coverage graphs showed 0% availability. This data-driven approach prevented a catastrophic inventory surplus. Our dual R&D strategy provided an immediate competitive edge with a superior "Tech 1" product, prepared a dominant "Tech 2" for the next round, and invested in long-term cost reduction.

Decision: We launched "Tech 1" with all 4 available features across all markets. We set a premium price in the US/Europe and a more aggressive, market-share-oriented price in Asia. Production in the US was maximized at 100% capacity.

Rationale: By offering a 4-feature product when competitors likely offered the standard 2, we immediately established a premium brand image. The tailored pricing strategy allowed us to convert this added value into revenue in mature markets while aggressively capturing customers in the high-growth Asian market. Maximizing in-house production was key to benefiting from the learning curve effect.

Decision: After stabilizing finances, we committed significant capital to begin construction of 4 new production plants in Asia (China).

Rationale: This was the most critical decision of the round. Our analysis identified the $27 per-unit export cost to Asia as our biggest profit drain. This investment will not only eliminate that cost but also create a more efficient global supply chain, with cheaper export routes to Europe and allowing our US facilities to focus solely on the domestic market. It transforms our greatest weakness into a formidable strength.

Decision: We secured $700 million in low-cost long-term debt to completely pay off over $660 million in high-interest emergency short-term debt. We also used transfer pricing to optimize our tax burden. We paid zero dividends.

Rationale: Paying dividends while on the verge of bankruptcy would have been irresponsible. Our decision was to save the company. The new debt not only cleaned our balance sheet of toxic, high-cost loans but also provided the "war chest" needed to fund our strategic investment in Asia. Every dollar was reinvested into building a more valuable company for the long term.
